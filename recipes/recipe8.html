Kacchi Biryani

Aromatic, flavorful, and rich — Kacchi Biryani is a royal dish from South Asia made with marinated mutton, fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of exotic spices. Every bite bursts with delicious layers of meat, rice, and aroma. Perfect for any celebration or feast!

Ingredients

  • 1 kg mutton (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 large onions (fried golden brown)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup mustard oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp biryani masala
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron soaked in warm milk
  • Fried potatoes (optional but traditional)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the meat: Mix mutton with yogurt, fried onions, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, biryani masala, salt, and oil. Cover and marinate overnight in the fridge for the best flavor.
  2. Prepare the rice: Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Parboil it with salt and whole spices until 70% cooked, then drain and keep aside.
  3. Layering: In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of marinated mutton, then add a layer of rice. Repeat layers and sprinkle saffron milk, ghee, and some fried onions on top.
  4. Seal and cook: Cover the pot tightly with dough or foil, then place a lid on top. Cook on low heat (“dum”) for 1 to 1.5 hours, until the meat is tender and the rice fully cooked.
  5. Serve: Gently mix before serving so the layers remain distinct. Serve hot with salad, raita, or boiled egg for the perfect royal meal!
