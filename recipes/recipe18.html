Fruit Custard

Fruit Custard is a creamy, chilled dessert made with smooth custard and a variety of fresh fruits. It's colorful, refreshing, and perfect for summer or after-dinner treats!

Ingredients


  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tbsp custard powder (vanilla flavor)
  • 3 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 apple (chopped)
  • 1 banana (sliced)
  • 1 mango (diced)
  • ½ cup grapes (cut in halves)
  • 1 orange (peeled & segmented)
  • Chopped dry fruits (cashew, pistachio, almonds) for garnish


Instructions


  1. Prepare custard base: Take 2 tbsp custard powder in a small bowl and mix with ¼ cup of cold milk to form a smooth paste.
  2. Boil milk: Heat the remaining milk in a pan. Once it starts to boil, add sugar and stir until dissolved.
  3. Add custard mixture: Lower the flame and slowly pour in the custard paste while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  4. Cook until thick: Keep stirring until the custard thickens and coats the spoon. Turn off the heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  5. Chill: Refrigerate the custard for at least 1–2 hours until nicely chilled.
  6. Prepare fruits: Chop all the fruits and keep them ready. Avoid watery fruits like watermelon for better texture.
  7. Combine: Just before serving, gently mix the chilled custard with all the fruits.
  8. Garnish & serve: Top with chopped cashew, pista, and almonds. Serve cold in dessert bowls or glasses.

Tip: For extra richness, you can add a few drops of vanilla essence or fold in some whipped cream before chilling. Make sure to add fruits only before serving — this keeps them fresh and crunchy!
