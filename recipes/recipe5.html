Beef Kala Bhuna

A rich and aromatic Bangladeshi beef dish slow-cooked in spices until dark, flavorful, and tender. Perfect with steamed rice or paratha!

Ingredients


  • 1 kg beef (cut into medium pieces)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 4-5 green chilies (optional, for extra heat)
  • 1/2 cup mustard or vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste


    • Instructions


    1. In a large bowl, mix beef with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili, cumin, coriander powder, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight gives best flavor).
    2. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or wok. Add sliced onions and fry until deep golden brown.
    3. Add the marinated beef and stir well. Cook over medium-high heat until the meat starts to release its juices and the oil begins to separate.
    4. Lower the heat, cover, and cook slowly, stirring occasionally, until the beef is tender and the gravy turns dark brown (“kala bhuna” literally means “black-fried”).
    5. Add green chilies and garam masala at the end. Fry for another 5-10 minutes until the oil rises to the top.
    6. Serve hot with steamed rice, pulao, or paratha. Enjoy the authentic taste of Bangladesh!
