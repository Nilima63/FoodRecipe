Halim

Halim is a rich, slow-cooked porridge made with wheat, lentils and tender meat — deeply flavorful and nourishing. Perfect for special occasions or a hearty meal, served with fried onions, lemon, and fresh herbs.

Ingredients


  • 500g beef or mutton (bone-in or boneless, cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup wheat (cracked wheat/dalia) or wheat berries
  • 1/2 cup chana dal (Bengal gram)
  • 1/2 cup moong dal (optional)
  • 2 large onions (sliced — for frying)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 4–6 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and fried onions for garnish
  • Lemon wedges and green chilies to serve


Instructions


  1. Soak and prep: Rinse the wheat and lentils. Soak wheat and chana dal for 1–2 hours to reduce cooking time.
  2. Cook the meat: In a large pot or pressure cooker, heat oil or ghee and fry half of the sliced onions until golden — set aside for garnish. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté briefly. Add the meat, turmeric, salt, and spices. Brown the meat for a few minutes.
  3. Add water and simmer: Add enough water to cover the meat. If using a pressure cooker, cook until the meat is tender (about 20–30 minutes). For pot cooking, simmer until meat is soft.
  4. Cook wheat & dals: In another pot (or same cooker if space allows), cook the soaked wheat and dals with water until very soft and breaking down. You may need to mash or blend lightly to get a smooth porridge texture.
  5. Combine: Once the meat is tender, remove bones (if used) and shred the meat. Add the shredded meat to the cooked wheat-dal mixture. Stir well and simmer together so flavors meld. Adjust consistency with hot water — halim should be thick and porridge-like.
  6. Final seasoning: Add cumin powder, red chili powder, and adjust salt. Cook on low heat for 10–15 minutes, stirring frequently to avoid sticking. For a richer taste, add a knob of butter or a splash of ghee.
  7. Serve: Ladle halim into bowls and top generously with fried onions, chopped coriander, lemon wedges, and green chilies. Serve hot with naan or paratha if you like.

Tip: For an extra silky texture, use an immersion blender briefly, or pass the mixture through a sieve. Slow-cooking and frequent stirring give the best Halim texture and flavor.
